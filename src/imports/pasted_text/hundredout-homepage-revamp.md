Revise the HundredOut homepage and simplify the structure. The current design has strong visual pieces, but the homepage still has too much content and too many sections competing for attention.

## Goal

Create a tighter, more effective homepage that:

* quickly explains what HundredOut is
* makes the app feel different
* highlights the social competition, side action, rewards, and crew energy
* feels fun, sharp, premium, and alive
* drives users toward download or deeper exploration

The homepage should feel like:
**a focused front door to the brand**
not:
**a full brand deck, full sitemap, or feature catalog**

## Core direction

Please reduce the homepage to a smaller set of stronger sections.

The homepage should stay centered on:

* the app
* social competition
* banter
* rivalry
* side action
* trophies / HUNNIDs / crew leaderboards
* the feeling of golf with your crew

Secondary brand elements like gear can exist, but should stay lighter and lower on the page.

## Updated homepage structure

Please revise the homepage around this flow:

1. **Header**
2. **Hero**
3. **How It Goes Down**
4. **Finally, an App That Gets It**
5. **Always More On The Line**
6. **Final CTA**
7. **Rep the Movement** (optional final teaser section before or near footer)

Footer should stay simple.

## Section guidance

### 1. Header

Keep it clean and premium:

* logo
* simple nav
* one primary CTA

Do not overload the header.

### 2. Hero

Keep the hero strong. It is one of the best parts of the current concept.

The hero should clearly communicate:

* golf with more action
* more banter
* more to play for

It should feel big, bold, social, competitive, and memorable.

Include:

* strong headline
* short supporting copy
* up to 2 CTA buttons
* supporting callouts if useful

Important:
If using supporting callouts, make sure they do not create redundancy with later sections.

### 3. How It Goes Down

Keep this section as the fast simplifier.

Its job is to make the app feel easy to understand:

* set it up
* play & compete
* own it

This section should be clean, clear, and visually light.
It should reduce friction and help visitors quickly understand the flow.

### 4. Finally, an App That Gets It

This should become the main “why HundredOut is different” section.

This section should absorb and communicate the most important differentiators:

* live competition
* crew leaderboards
* trophies
* HUNNIDs
* choose your vibe / language tolerance
* rivalry
* bragging rights
* social golf energy

Important:
This section should carry more weight so other sections can be removed.

Do not split these ideas into multiple separate homepage sections unless absolutely necessary.
This should be the core product-difference section.

### 5. Always More On The Line

Keep this as a dedicated section because it is one of the clearest and most ownable differentiators.

Important nuance:
This should not feel like a gambling site.

It should communicate that HundredOut helps people play for:

* cash
* drinks
* dares
* pushups
* bragging rights
* funny punishments
* whatever the group wants

The message should be:
**there is always more to play for**
not:
**this is a betting product**

Also make clear that:

* users handle settling up themselves
* HundredOut tracks the side action
* the spirit is fun, not gambling

### 6. Final CTA

Keep the final CTA simple, strong, and emotionally clear.

It should push toward:

* download
* exploring the app
* taking the next step into the HundredOut ecosystem

This should feel like a close, not another info block.

### 7. Rep the Movement

This can remain as the last lifestyle/brand teaser section.

That is okay.

But keep it lighter than the core app sections.
It should feel like:

* a stylish final nod to the broader brand
* a bridge into gear / lifestyle
* a closing expression of identity

It should not pull focus from the app story or feel like a full detour.

## Sections to cut or merge

Please remove or consolidate sections that are no longer needed as full homepage modules.

Specifically:

* **cut “More to Play For” as a standalone full section**
* **cut or heavily reduce “It’s About the Moments”**
* do not create too many separate bands explaining trophies, HUNNIDs, crew boards, and social energy independently

Instead, fold those ideas into the stronger sections above.

## Tone and copy direction

The homepage should feel:

* fun
* sharp
* confident
* competitive
* social
* premium
* alive

Use language around:

* your crew
* rivalry
* bragging rights
* trophies
* HUNNIDs
* side action
* banter
* chaos
* golf stories people keep bringing up later
* more to play for than just score

Avoid language that feels:

* corporate
* startup-ish
* SaaS-like
* stat-heavy
* overly polished in a serious way
* generic golf marketing

## Design guidance

Keep the current strong visual foundation:

* bold typography
* dark hero / dark key sections
* strong contrast
* black / white / coral-red / blue / green accents
* premium spacing
* confident modern layout

But improve:

* pacing
* hierarchy
* clarity
* focus
* section-to-section momentum

The page should feel leaner and more intentional.

## Deliverable

Please revise the homepage concept so it uses fewer, stronger sections and tells a clearer story.

The final homepage should feel like:

* this is a different kind of golf app
* this is built for real friend-group golf
* this makes the round more social and competitive
* this brand has personality
* I get it quickly
* I want to download it or explore more

A good guiding line is:
**hook them, explain the difference, show the fun, and get them to the next step**
