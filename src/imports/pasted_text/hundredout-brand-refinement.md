Refine this HundredOut website direction so it feels **more fun, more social, more competitive, and more true to the brand**, while keeping the current strong visual foundation.

## What is already working

* The visual system already feels bold, modern, premium, and high energy
* The typography is strong
* The dark sections, grid background, glow effects, and accent colors are working
* The homepage hero direction is close, especially ideas like:

  * “Golf with More Action”
  * “More Banter”
  * “Where Golf Gets Social”
  * “Designed for Competition”

## What needs to change

Right now the site still feels a little too:

* sports-tech
* corporate
* stat-heavy
* polished in a serious way
* generic in some of the section copy
* too focused on premium / membership / features instead of the real feeling of the brand

It needs to feel more like:

* golf with your crew
* side bets
* banter
* bragging rights
* pressure moments
* laughs
* chaos
* competition
* rewards
* memorable rounds

This should feel like a **modern golf brand with a pulse**, not a generic startup landing page and not a country club brand.

## Core brand truth

HundredOut is about making golf:

* more social
* more competitive
* more fun
* more expressive
* more memorable

The website should sell the **energy of the round** and the **culture around it**, not just explain app features.

## Important product / brand elements that must be represented

Make sure the updated site direction includes these important parts of the HundredOut ecosystem:

### 1. Trophies

Trophies are a core part of the experience.
They create their own layer of competition, identity, progression, and bragging rights.
They should feel like more than badges. They should feel collectible, competitive, and culturally meaningful inside the HundredOut world.

### 2. Crew Leaderboards

Crew leaderboards matter because they turn friend groups into ongoing competition.
This is not just about one round. It is about status within your crew, low rounds, ongoing rivalry, and social pressure.

### 3. HUNNIDs

HUNNIDs are in-app reward tokens earned through play.
“HUNNID” is slang for hundred and is part of the HundredOut brand language.

These tokens should be presented as:

* a gamified reward layer
* something that adds motivation and fun
* part of the brand’s future ecosystem

Important:
The future vision is that HUNNIDs will eventually connect to a marketplace or reward redemption layer, but that does **not** need to be oversold right now. It can be hinted at as part of the broader future brand world.

### 4. Betting is not just about money

HundredOut supports betting and side action, but the brand should not come across like a gambling product.

Important nuance:

* users can bet money
* the platform does **not** process the money transactions
* the bigger story is that HundredOut helps people bet on **fun**, not just cash

The app supports side action around things like:

* drinks
* dares
* pushups
* bragging rights
* funny punishments
* custom stakes between friends

That spirit should come through in the website.
It should feel like:
**there is always more to play for**
not:
**this is a gambling app**

## Key messaging shift

Move away from generic proof-point and startup-style stat energy.

Use less of:

* inflated-looking stats
* generic “premium feature” language
* safe phrases like “built for the modern golfer”
* membership/pricing layouts that feel like SaaS or fantasy sports
* feature cards that could belong to any sports app

Use more of:

* emotional, social, and competitive storytelling
* language about the round, the crew, and what happens between people
* copy that feels playful, confident, sharp, and slightly edgy
* sections that highlight moments, status, rewards, rivalry, and culture

## Language tolerance / vibe setting

A key part of the app is that users can choose the tone of the app experience:

* family-friendly / clean
* more explicit / adult / unfiltered

Do **not** present this in a crude or gimmicky way.
Instead, weave it into the brand as a smart, memorable differentiator.

Good framings:

* “Choose your vibe.”
* “Keep it clean or turn things up.”
* “Set the tone for your crew.”
* “From family-friendly to a little more unfiltered.”

This should feel like a subtle but ownable part of the brand personality, not the main headline of the site.
