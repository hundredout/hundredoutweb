Build a revised **HundredOut homepage UI** that is tighter, more focused, and more emotionally compelling than the current version.

## Overall objective

Design a homepage that quickly communicates:

* what HundredOut is
* why it feels different
* why it is more social, more competitive, and more fun than a typical golf app
* why someone should keep exploring or download the app

The homepage should feel like the **front door to the brand**, not a full sitemap, full sales deck, or full product catalog.

## Core design priority

The homepage needs stronger prioritization.

It should primarily sell:

* the app
* social competition
* crew energy
* side action
* fun, banter, rivalry, and memorable golf moments

It should only lightly preview secondary parts of the business like:

* gear
* club / membership

Do not give everything equal visual weight.

## Required homepage structure

Design the homepage around this tighter flow:

1. Header
2. Hero
3. How It Works
4. Why HundredOut / Core App Experience
5. More On The Line / Side Action
6. Moments / Brand Energy
7. Gear teaser or Club teaser
8. Final CTA

Footer stays simple and supportive.

## Section-by-section instructions

### 1. Header

Create a clean fixed header with:

* HundredOut logo
* simple nav
* one primary CTA

Keep it clean and premium.
Do not overload the header.

### 2. Hero

This is the main hook.

The hero should:

* immediately communicate the brand energy
* feel bold, social, competitive, and modern
* position HundredOut as golf with more action, banter, and something on the line

Include:

* strong headline
* short subhead
* 2 CTAs max
* 3 supporting callouts under the hero if helpful

The hero should feel emotional and ownable, not generic sports-tech.

### 3. How It Works

Use one clean, easy-to-scan section that simplifies the experience fast.

This section should make the product feel approachable.
Keep it visually simple and not overly dense.

Likely structure:

* setup
* play / compete
* flex / track / own it

This section should reduce friction and help new visitors understand the flow.

### 4. Why HundredOut / Core App Experience

This is the main “why this app matters” section.

Combine these ideas into one strong section:

* app features
* rewards
* some social differentiators

Include these product elements inside this section:

* trophies
* HUNNIDs
* crew leaderboards
* choose your vibe / language tolerance
* side action
* rivalry / status / bragging rights

Important:
These should feel woven together into one strong experience story.
Do not break them into too many small cards or mini-sections.

The goal is to communicate:
**this app makes the round feel more alive**

### 5. More On The Line / Side Action

Give side action its own section because it is one of the clearest differentiators.

Important nuance:
This should not feel like a gambling website.

Communicate that HundredOut helps people play for:

* money
* drinks
* dares
* pushups
* bragging rights
* funny punishments
* whatever the group wants

The point is:
**there is always more to play for**
not:
**this is a betting product**

This section should feel playful, sharp, and social.

### 6. Moments / Brand Energy

This section should sell the emotional side of HundredOut.

Build around ideas like:

* comebacks
* meltdowns
* banter
* crew stories
* rivalry
* pressure moments
* laughs after bad holes
* stories that carry past 18

This section gives the site soul.
It should feel more expressive and human than the more structured product sections.

### 7. Gear teaser or Club teaser

Do not make this a full heavyweight homepage section.

Choose one lighter teaser block:

* Gear, if the goal is to build lifestyle energy
  or
* Club, if the goal is to support membership / insider identity

This section should be short, stylish, and secondary.

### 8. Final CTA

End with a clean, focused CTA block.

Goal:
push users toward:

* downloading the app
* exploring the app page
* entering the brand ecosystem

This should feel like a strong close, not another information section.

## Visual direction

Keep the strong visual foundation from the current concept:

* bold, premium typography
* dark sections
* strong contrast
* energetic accent colors
* modern layout system
* clean but expressive composition

But reduce anything that feels:

* too corporate
* too stat-heavy
* too membership-sales-heavy
* too startup-ish
* too feature-grid-heavy

## Design behavior guidelines

* Reduce total homepage density
* Give fewer sections more impact
* Create stronger pacing from section to section
* Vary layouts, but keep the system cohesive
* Use section hierarchy clearly so visitors always know what matters most
* Make the app and social competition the center of gravity

## Brand tone to reflect visually

The design should feel:

* fun
* sharp
* confident
* premium
* social
* competitive
* alive

It should feel like:
**your crew, your game, your level of chaos**

## Specific product/story elements that must appear somewhere

These still need to be represented in the homepage, but not as separate standalone sections unless absolutely necessary:

* trophies
* crew leaderboards
* HUNNIDs
* side action beyond money
* choose your vibe / language tolerance
* golf with your crew
* bragging rights
* more to play for than just score

## Things to avoid

Avoid homepage design that feels like:

* a product catalog
* a SaaS site
* a fantasy sports site
* a country club golf brand
* a fake-big-company startup landing page
* a page overloaded with cards, metrics, and equal-weight sections

## Deliverable expectations

Create a revised homepage concept that has:

* fewer sections
* stronger visual hierarchy
* better prioritization
* more emotional clarity
* clearer brand story
* a better balance of product and personality

The homepage should make people feel:

* this is different
* this is fun
* this is social
* this is competitive
* this is a real brand
* I get it quickly
* I want to explore more
